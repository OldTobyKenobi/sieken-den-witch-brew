# Chiptune Learning Curriculum

Tailored for a complete beginner targeting SNES-style and modern hybrid chiptune. Starting point: basic music theory knowledge, very beginner DAW experience, MIDI keyboard available.

---

## Phase 1: Music Theory Basics (3–4 weeks)

You don't need much — just enough to write melodies and chord progressions intentionally.

- **musictheory.net** — free, self-paced lessons. Work through lessons 1–15.
- **8-Bit Music Theory** (YouTube) — game-music-specific theory. Watch the fundamentals playlist. Essential for this style.
- **Goal:** Understand major/minor scales, basic chord progressions (I-IV-V-I), and rhythm/time signatures.

---

## Phase 2: Chiptune Constraints First (2–3 weeks)

Before touching a full DAW, learn *inside the box* — it forces you to think like a chiptune composer.

- **BeepBox** (beepbox.co) — free, browser-based, zero setup. Very constrained on purpose. Spend a couple of weeks making short loops here.
- Learn what square waves, triangle waves, sawtooth, and noise sound like and what each is used for.

---

## Phase 3: Move to a Real DAW (ongoing)

For SNES/modern hybrid with MIDI keyboard support:

- **LMMS** — free, cross-platform, beginner-friendly. Plug in your MIDI keyboard and start using the piano roll.
- Add the free VST plugin **MAGICAL 8BIT PLUG** for authentic chiptune waveforms inside LMMS.
- For SNES-style specifically, **Furnace Tracker** (free) supports the actual SNES sound chip (SPC700). Steeper learning curve — come here once you're comfortable with Phase 2.

---

## Phase 4: Study the Source Material (parallel to Phase 3)

- Pull up the Chrono Trigger and Secret of Mana OSTs and listen analytically — how many simultaneous voices, what carries the melody vs. bass vs. rhythm, how tracks loop cleanly.
- **OverClocked ReMix** (ocremix.org) — community of game music arrangers. Great for hearing what's possible and reading about techniques.

---

## Phase 5: Game-Ready Composition (ongoing)

- Learn how to write **looping music** — tracks that repeat seamlessly with no audible seam.
- Learn **stingers and transitions** — short musical cues for events (item found, boss appears, level up, etc.).
- Godot has a solid audio system that hooks into this later — no need to worry about it yet.

## Notes

- **MIDI keyboard:** Plug directly into LMMS for real-time note input — a significant advantage over clicking notes into a piano roll.
- **Banjo & ukulele:** Recording live folk instruments and blending with chiptune is a genuinely distinctive sound that fits the game's whimsical tone. Worth experimenting with once comfortable in a DAW.
- **No AI audio** — all music and SFX for the game will be human-created assets.
